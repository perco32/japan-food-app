What is Zaru Soba?

A classic cold noodle dish and a staple of Japanese summers. After being boiled, the soba noodles are chilled and served on a "zaru," a traditional flat bamboo tray.

Ingredients (for 1 serving)

  • Dried soba noodles: 100g (approx. 3.5 oz)
  • Mentsuyu (noodle soup base, 3x concentrate): 50ml (approx. 3.4 tbsp)
  • Cold water: 100ml (approx. 0.42 US cups)

Favorite Condiments (Yakumi)

  • Chopped green onions
  • Wasabi
  • Shredded nori seaweed

Instructions

  1. Boil the Soba: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Cook the soba noodles according to the package directions.
  2. Rinse and Chill: Drain the cooked soba in a colander. Gently rinse under cold running water to remove excess starch. Finally, plunge the noodles into a bowl of ice water for about 10 seconds to make them perfectly firm, then drain well.

    Tip: This is the most crucial step for achieving the firm texture and smooth feel that is key to delicious zaru soba.

  3. Prepare the Dipping Sauce: In a small cup, mix the mentsuyu and cold water.
  4. Serve: Arrange the chilled soba on a zaru or a plate and top with shredded nori. Serve with the dipping sauce and condiments on the side. Dip a small amount of noodles into the sauce to eat.
