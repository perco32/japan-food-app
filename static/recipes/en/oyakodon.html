What is Oyakodon?

This dish gets its unique name, "Parent-and-Child Bowl," because it features both chicken ("parent") and egg ("child") simmered together. It's a classic and heartwarming Japanese home-cooked meal.

Ingredients (for 1 serving)

  • Chicken thigh (boneless): 1/2 piece (approx. 120g / 4 oz)
  • Onion: 1/4
  • Eggs: 2
  • Warm cooked rice: 1 large bowl
  • Optional garnish: Mitsuba (Japanese parsley) or shredded nori

☆ Warishita (Simmering Broth)

  • Dashi stock: 100ml (approx. 0.42 US cups) - Can be made with 100ml water and 1/3 tsp dashi powder.
  • Soy sauce: 2 tbsp
  • Mirin (sweet rice wine): 2 tbsp
  • Sugar: 1 tsp

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Cut the chicken into small, bite-sized pieces. Slice the onion thinly. In a bowl, lightly beat the eggs—the key is to just break the yolk and barely mix, not to whisk completely.
  2. Simmer: In a small skillet, combine the ☆ broth ingredients and the sliced onion. Bring to a simmer over medium heat. Add the chicken and cook for 2-3 minutes until it's cooked through.
  3. First Egg Pour: Slowly pour about two-thirds of the beaten egg mixture in a circular motion over the chicken and onions. Cover with a lid and cook on low heat for 30-60 seconds, until the egg is partially set.
  4. Second Egg Pour: Remove the lid and pour the remaining one-third of the egg into the center.
  5. Finish: Cover again and let it steam for about 10 seconds. Turn off the heat. Gently slide the entire mixture over a bowl of hot rice. Garnish with mitsuba if desired, and serve immediately.
