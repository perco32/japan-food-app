Ingredients (for 2 servings)

  • Pork loin chops (for tonkatsu): 2 slices (200g / approx. 7 oz)
  • Beaten egg: 1
  • Panko (Japanese breadcrumbs): as needed
  • Salt and pepper: a pinch
  • All-purpose flour: 3 tbsp
  • Vegetable oil: enough to fill 4cm / 1.5 inches from the bottom of the pot

Garnish

  • Shredded cabbage: 2 leaves (80g)
  • Cherry tomatoes: 4

Other

  • Karashi (Japanese mustard): to taste
  • Tonkatsu sauce: to taste

Instructions

  1. Make about 5 small incisions along the line between the fat and the red meat of the pork.
    Tip: This helps prevent the pork from curling up as it cooks.
  2. Season both sides of the pork with salt and pepper, then lightly coat with all-purpose flour.
  3. Dip the pork into the beaten egg, then press firmly into the panko, ensuring an even coating.
    Tip: A complete coating helps keep the meat juicy and prevents the breading from falling off.
  4. Pour vegetable oil into a pot to a depth of about 4cm (1.5 inches) and heat to 170°C (340°F). Add the pork and fry for about 2 minutes. Flip it over and fry for another 2-3 minutes until golden brown and cooked through.
    Tip: 170°C is when fine bubbles steadily rise from the tip of a wooden chopstick inserted in the oil. Avoid touching the pork right after putting it in, as the coating can easily come off.
  5. Cut the tonkatsu into bite-sized strips. Serve on a plate with the garnish, and add tonkatsu sauce and karashi mustard to your liking.
    Tip: Press the knife down firmly from above for clean cuts.
