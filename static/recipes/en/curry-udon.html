About Curry Udon

A popular dish that combines two Japanese national favorites: curry rice and udon noodles. It features a slightly sweet, dashi-flavored curry soup that warms you up. Be careful not to splash the soup on white clothes!

Ingredients (for 1 serving)

  • Frozen udon noodle block: 1
  • Thinly sliced pork belly: 50g (approx. 1.8 oz) - Chicken or beef also work well.
  • Onion: 1/4
  • Japanese curry roux block: 1 piece (approx. 20g / 0.7 oz)
  • Mentsuyu (noodle soup base, 3x concentrate): 2 tbsp
  • Water: 300ml (approx. 1.25 US cups)
  • Vegetable oil: a little
  • Chopped green onions: for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Slice the onion thinly. Cut the pork into bite-sized pieces. Finely chop the curry roux block to help it dissolve easily.
  2. Sauté: Heat the vegetable oil in a small pot and sauté the pork and onion.
  3. Simmer: When the meat changes color, add the water and mentsuyu and bring to a boil. Skim off any scum, then simmer on low heat for 3-4 minutes.
  4. Dissolve the Roux: Turn off the heat completely. Add the chopped curry roux and stir until it has fully dissolved.

    Tip: Turning off the heat before adding the roux is the key to a smooth, lump-free soup.

  5. Finish: Turn the heat back on to low and simmer for 1-2 minutes, stirring, until the soup thickens. Meanwhile, cook the udon and place it in a bowl. Pour the hot curry soup over the udon, garnish with green onions, and serve.
